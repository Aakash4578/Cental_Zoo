Fun Fact

Giraffes are the tallest land animals, known for their long necks and legs. They are fascinating creatures with unique adaptations that help them thrive in their environment.

habitat

Giraffes are herbivores, meaning they only eat plant material. Their diet primarily consists of leaves from tall trees, especially acacia trees, which they can reach using their long necks and prehensile tongues.

Giraffes have specialized adaptations to help them feed on leaves at greater heights than other animals.

Their long necks and tongues, which can extend up to 45 centimeters (18 inches), allow them to reach leaves that are out of reach for most herbivores. They have tough, abrasive tongues that help them grip and strip leaves from branches, while their lips are thick and flexible to avoid prickles and thorns.
