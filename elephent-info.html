Fun Fact

Fun Fact: Elephants are the largest land animals on Earth. They can reach an impressive height of up to 13 feet (4 meters) at the shoulder and weigh between 5,000 and 14,000 pounds (2,268 to 6,350 kilograms)!

habitat

Elephants are found in various habitats across Africa and Asia. African elephants can be found in savannas, forests, and grasslands, while Asian elephants are typically found in tropical rainforests and grasslands.

Diet

Elephants are herbivores, which means they eat plants. They have a diverse diet that consists mainly of grasses, leaves, bark, fruits, and roots.

Elephants are the largest land animals on Earth.

They inhabit diverse environments, such as African savannas, forests, and grasslands, or Asian rainforests and grasslands. These herbivorous creatures have a varied diet comprising grasses, leaves, bark, fruits, and roots. With their remarkable size and impressive appetite, elephants are truly magnificent creatures.
