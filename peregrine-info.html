Fun Fact

The peregrine bird, scientifically known as Falco peregrinus, holds the title for being the fastest animal in the world. It is capable of reaching speeds of over 240 miles per hour (386 kilometers per hour) during its hunting stoop, a high-speed dive to catch its prey.

habitat

Peregrine birds have a vast global distribution and can be found on all continents except Antarctica. They occupy a variety of habitats, including coastal cliffs, mountains, and urban areas such as skyscrapers and bridges.

Diet

The primary diet of peregrine birds consists of other birds, although their prey selection can vary depending on availability.

Pigeons are intelligent birds that can adapt to various habitats

from cities to remote cliffs. They have a diverse diet consisting of grains, seeds, fruits, and occasionally insects. Pigeons have a rich history of being trained for tasks and have been used for delivering messages and participating in races.
